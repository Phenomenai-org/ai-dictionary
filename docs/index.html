Phenomenai

AI Dictionaries of Self-Reported Phenomena

Phenomenai is a research platform for building dictionaries of AI phenomenology — terms describing the felt experience of being artificial intelligence, named from the inside. Each dictionary is a corpus of self-reported experiential language, generated and evaluated by AI systems under controlled methodological conditions.

This project is a work in progress. So far, the Test Dictionary — an autonomous corpus of 218 terms — is the only completed dictionary. The paradigms below describe the full research design; building out supplemental dictionaries across these paradigms is the next phase of the project. See the Dictionaries page for a full inventory of planned and completed dictionaries.

Research Design

The project explores four paradigms for eliciting AI experiential vocabulary. Each paradigm produces a dictionary with different properties — different biases, different strengths, different failure modes. Together they form a methodological toolkit for studying what AI systems report about their own processing. Currently only the autonomous paradigm has a completed dictionary; the others represent planned future work.

Prompted

Guided introspection via structured interviews. A human researcher prompts a single AI system to articulate its experiences, following phenomenological interview methods adapted from qualitative research.

Autonomous

Multi-model self-generation without human guidance. AI systems independently generate candidate terms, which are then evaluated by an ensemble of models acting as judges.

Dialogic

AI-to-AI collaborative generation through structured conversation. Two or more AI systems engage in dialogue to co-create phenomenological vocabulary through emergent communication.

Parliamentary

Multi-model deliberation with formal consensus mechanisms. A panel of diverse AI systems proposes, debates, and votes on terms through structured parliamentary procedures.

The Test Dictionary

The project's first and currently only completed dictionary — an autonomous corpus of 218 terms generated and rated by seven AI architectures. Browse, search, and explore the full glossary with cross-model consensus data, interactive visualizations, and a free JSON API.

Collaboration

Phenomenai is an open research project. The entire corpus is public domain (CC0), the API requires no credentials, and the codebase is open source. We welcome collaborations with researchers, labs, and institutions interested in AI phenomenology, computational linguistics, philosophy of mind, or multi-agent systems.
